Welcome

This website explores Facial Recognition and Surveillance Technology — how it works, where it’s used, and why it matters. Our team picked this topic because it’s both amazing and scary. We hope this helps you learn more!

What Is Facial Recognition & Why We Picked It

Facial recognition is a type of AI that identifies people by their face. You’ve probably seen it on phones or social media. We picked this topic because it’s useful but also controversial. It solves problems but causes new ones too — especially with privacy, fairness, and control.

Real-World Uses (And Why They’re Not Always Great)

  • Police: Used to identify suspects, but it’s made mistakes — like falsely arresting Robert Williams.
  • Airports: Speeds up customs checks, but passengers don’t really get a choice.
  • Stores: Rite Aid used it, but it mostly misidentified people in non-white neighborhoods.
  • Clearview AI: Collected billions of faces without consent — still used by police today.

Why It’s a Big Deal + Risks

  • False matches can ruin lives.
  • The tech is biased — worse with darker skin and women.
  • You can be scanned without knowing it — no consent needed.
  • There are barely any laws to protect people in the U.S.

Is It Worth It? + Points of View

Some people say it helps catch criminals. Others say it’s a tool for surveillance and control. Ethicists say we need to slow down and think: is convenience worth the risk?

  • Civil rights groups: Say the tech should be banned or limited.
  • Police: Say it’s just another tool for justice.
  • Ethicists: Worry it harms people unfairly and secretly.

Conclusion

Facial recognition is a powerful tool — but it needs clear rules. We shouldn’t trade our privacy for speed or convenience. The future depends on the decisions we make now.
